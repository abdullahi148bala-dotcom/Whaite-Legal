Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2025  ·  Effective: June 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how Whaite ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal data when you use the Whaite mobile application. It is governed by the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA) and the General Application and Implementation Directive (GAID) 2025 issued by the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC). By using Whaite, you consent to the practices described here.

1. Who We Are

Whaite is a social networking application that allows users to connect, share content, engage in real-time chat, participate in mini-games, and build reputation through the WhaiteScore system. Whaite acts as a Data Controller for personal data collected through the app.

For data-related enquiries, contact us at: TheWhaiteApp@protonmail.com

2. Data We Collect

We only collect data that is necessary to provide you with the Whaite experience. The categories of data we collect are:

Account & Identity Data

  • Your name, email address, and profile photo — obtained via Google Sign-In (OAuth 2.0)
  • Username and display name you choose during onboarding
  • Biography (bio) text you optionally provide
  • Account type (public or private)

Content Data

  • Posts, images, and videos you upload to the app (stored on Cloudinary)
  • Comments and replies you write
  • Direct messages and chat content you send to other users
  • Stickers and reactions sent in chats
  • Mini-game moves and results (TicTacToe, WordLadder, Minesweeper, DotsConnect)

Activity & Reputation Data

  • Likes, follows, shares, and interactions you make or receive
  • WhaiteScore points earned through your activity on the platform
  • Pulse streaks and flame events tied to your chat activity
  • Ad watch events (if applicable)

Technical Data

  • Device type and operating system (collected by Firebase automatically)
  • App usage data and crash reports (via Firebase Analytics / Crashlytics)
  • Authentication tokens managed by Firebase Authentication

We do not collect your phone number, precise GPS location, contacts list, or payment information.

3. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the NDPA 2023, we process your personal data on the following lawful bases:

  • Consent — you agree to this policy when you sign in to Whaite for the first time.
  • Contract performance — processing necessary to provide you with the Whaite service as described in our Terms of Service.
  • Legitimate interests — improving app performance, detecting abuse, preventing fraud, and ensuring the safety of all users.
  • Legal obligation — where we are required to process or retain data to comply with applicable laws.

4. How We Use Your Data

  • To create and manage your Whaite account
  • To display your profile, posts, and content to other users
  • To enable real-time messaging, reactions, and game interactions
  • To calculate and update your WhaiteScore
  • To send in-app notifications (likes, follows, comments, messages)
  • To detect and prevent spam, abuse, and violations of our Terms of Service
  • To comply with legal obligations and respond to lawful requests
  • To improve and debug the application

We do not sell your personal data to third parties. We do not use your data to build advertising profiles for external platforms.

5. Third-Party Services

Whaite uses the following third-party services that may process your personal data as part of delivering the app:

  • Google Firebase — authentication, real-time database (Firestore), cloud storage, and crash analytics. Google's privacy policy applies: policies.google.com/privacy
  • Cloudinary — cloud-based image and video storage and delivery for user-uploaded media. Cloudinary's privacy policy applies: cloudinary.com/privacy
  • Google Sign-In (OAuth 2.0) — used for account creation and login. We only receive the data Google provides with your consent: name, email, and profile photo.

Each of these services has its own privacy policy, and we recommend you review them. We select third-party providers who maintain appropriate data protection standards.

6. Data Sharing

We do not share your personal data with third parties except in the following circumstances:

  • Other users — content you post publicly (profile, posts, comments) is visible to other Whaite users according to your privacy settings. Direct messages are only visible to the intended recipient.
  • Service providers — Firebase and Cloudinary as described above, acting as data processors on our behalf.
  • Legal requirements — if required to do so by law, court order, or governmental authority in Nigeria or another applicable jurisdiction.
  • Safety — where we reasonably believe disclosure is necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of Whaite, our users, or the public.

7. Cross-Border Data Transfers

Your data may be stored on servers located outside Nigeria (including servers operated by Google and Cloudinary in the United States and other regions). Where we transfer personal data outside Nigeria, we take steps to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place consistent with the NDPA 2023 and the GAID 2025, including contractual obligations with our service providers.

8. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account:

  • Your profile data is removed from Firestore within 30 days
  • Your uploaded media is removed from Cloudinary within 30 days
  • Your messages may remain on the recipient's device until they also delete them
  • Anonymised or aggregated data may be retained for analytics purposes and cannot be used to identify you

We may retain certain data longer where required by applicable law or for legitimate security/fraud prevention purposes.

9. Your Rights Under the NDPA 2023

As a data subject under Nigerian law, you have the following rights:

  • Right to access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure — request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to data portability — receive your data in a structured, commonly used format
  • Right to withdraw consent — withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to lodge a complaint — file a complaint with the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC) at ndpc.gov.ng

To exercise any of these rights, email us at TheWhaiteApp@protonmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.

10. Account Deletion

You may delete your Whaite account at any time from within the app under Settings → Delete Account. You may also request deletion by emailing TheWhaiteApp@protonmail.com. Upon deletion, your account and associated personal data will be removed in accordance with Section 8 above.

11. Children's Privacy

Whaite is not intended for users under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has created an account, please contact us immediately at TheWhaiteApp@protonmail.com and we will delete the account promptly.

Users between 13 and 17 may use Whaite with parental or guardian awareness. We comply with Google Play's Child Safety Standards Policy for social applications.

12. Data Security

We take the security of your personal data seriously. Our technical and organisational measures include:

  • Firebase Security Rules to restrict data access to authorised users only
  • HTTPS/TLS encryption for all data in transit
  • Google OAuth 2.0 for secure authentication (we never store passwords)
  • Cloudinary secure upload tokens and access controls for media
  • Role-based access controls for any administrative operations

No system is 100% secure. In the event of a data breach that poses a risk to your rights, we will notify affected users and the NDPC within the timeframes required by the NDPA 2023.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will notify you through the app and update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
